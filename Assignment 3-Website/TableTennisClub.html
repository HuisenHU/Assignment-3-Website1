Table Tennis Rules

Each table tennis game has 11 points. Players on each side of the game serve two balls in turn. After serving, the serve is exchanged. Whoever reaches 11 points first wins the game and proceeds to the next game. However, when the score of the two players reaches 10:10, a play-off game is required, that is, each player on both sides serves a ball in turn, until one side exceeds the other by two points and wins. The general competition is divided into three best-of-three (preliminary), three-best-of-five (semi-final), best-of-seven (final), three-best-of-five and best-of-seven. In the tiebreaker, that is, the last game after the big score is 2-2 or 3-3, when one side wins by 5 points, the players from both sides need to exchange venues. Singles rules: After finishing the selection and scoring, let's introduce the rules of the game. In singles, one party can serve from any position on its own table, and the direction and location of the ball are not limited. Famous table tennis player

Yining Zhang

Nationality: Chinese

Yining Zhang, born in 1981, is a world-renowned table tennis player and one of the five grand slam table tennis players in China. She started playing at the age of 6 and entered the national team at the age of 12. She is the champion of individual World Championships, Olympic Games and World Cup. Consistent player.

Linghui Kong

Nationality: Chinese

Linghui Kong was born in Harbin, Heilongjiang Province in 1975. He is the winner of the World Table Tennis Championships, World Cup and Olympic table tennis men's singles "Grand Slam", one of the five Chinese table tennis Grand Slam players, known as the "Ping Pong Prince".

Jan Nova Waldnell

Nationality: Sweden

Jan Nova Waldnell, born in 1965, is a famous Swedish table tennis player and the first table tennis Grand Slam player in the world who has won the Olympic Games, World Table Tennis Championships, World Cup and European Championships. , He plays very artistic and changeable, so he is also known as "Mozart in table tennis".
