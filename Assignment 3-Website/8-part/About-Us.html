INTRODUCTION OF TABLE TENNIS:

Table tennis is a popular ball sport in the world, including offense, confrontation and defense. Table tennis originated in England. In January 1926, an international table tennis tournament was held in Berlin, Germany. A total of 64 male athletes from 9 countries participated in the competition. In December of the same year, the International Table Tennis Federation was formally established, and the European Championships held in London were named the first World Table Tennis Championships.

Table tennis originated in England. At the end of the 19th century, tennis was popular in Europe. However, due to the limitation of venues and weather, some college students in the UK moved tennis indoors, using the dining table as the table, the book as the net, and the parchment as the racket, playing around on the dining table. In 1890, the British athlete Gibb brought back some celluloid balls as toys for table tennis. Several British naval officers stationed in India stumbled upon the thrill of playing tennis on a small table. Later, they switched to solid rubber instead of a solid ball with little elasticity, and then changed to a hollow plastic ball, and replaced the racket with a wooden board, and played this novel "tennis game" on the table, so Table tennis was born.

ABOUT THE RACKET:

Types of Table Tennis Rubber positive rubber: also called short-grain rubber, which is characterized by good elasticity, fast speed, stable hitting, and no eating and turning. One of the most common types of rubber used by athletes. If you feel that your wrists are flexible, but your arms and waist are not strong enough, it is best to choose a positive rubber racket that wins with speed.

Types of Table Tennis Rubber Reverse Rubber: It is characterized by strong rotation, stable and easy control, and is suitable for arc type or arc combined with fast attack style. It is also the preferred type for beginners because of its stable hitting and good control of the ball. For professional players, there are a variety of reverse rubber sponge rackets with different properties to choose from, such as strong rubber skin and soft and thick matching sponge, so they have good "bite" ball and "wrap" ball characteristics. Reverse rubber sponge rubber is the most commonly used rubber for straight and horizontal shooters. Almost 100% of European players use this rubber, and nearly 80% of Asian players use it.

Types of Table Tennis Rubber Raw rubber: Raw rubber is rubber with upward particles and a diameter greater than its height. It is characterized by the sinking of the ball and the weak rotation of the ball. The ball is especially easy to control because the raw rubber has a spin-reducing effect. At present, a group of players who attack from both sides of the horizontal shot have adopted a combination of forehand rubber (pulling the arc circle) and backhand raw rubber (quick dial and short swing) in order to obtain the change of the shot.

Types of Table Tennis Rubber Anti-Arc Rubber: There is also a kind of anti-adhesive sponge rubber called "Anti-Arc Rubber" specially for the arc ball. The speed is slow, the arc is short, and it sinks and erratic after landing, making it difficult for opponents to judge according to routine, especially effective against arc balls
